2.7 Exercises

  • 2.1 Let \((S, \Sigma )\) be a measurable space. Show that for all \(A, B \in \Sigma \)

    • (a) \({\1}_{A \cup B} = {\1}_{A} + {\1}_{B} - {\1}_{A \cap B}\),

    • (b) \({\1}_{A^{c}} = 1 - {\1}_{A}\),

    • (c) \({\1}_{A - B} = {\1}_{A} - {\1}_{B}\), if \(B \subseteq A\),

    • (d) \({\1}_{A \cap B} = {\1}_{A}{\1}_{B}.\)

    Furthermore if \((A_{n})\) is a sequence of disjoint sets in \(\Sigma \) and \(A = \bigcup _{n=1}^{\infty }A_{n}\), show that \({\1}_{A} = \sum _{n=1}^{\infty } {\1}_{A_{n}}\).

    Hint: In (a) consider what happens to both sides in each of the four cases: \(x \in A, x \in B; x \in A, x \notin B\), etc.

  • 2.2 Let \((a_{n})\) and \((b_{n})\) be bounded sequences of real numbers. Show that

    • (a) \(\ls a_{n} = -\li (- a_{n})\),

    • (b) \(\ls (a_{n} + b_{n}) \leq \ls a_{n} + \ls b_{n}\),

    • (c) \(\li (a_{n} + b_{n}) \geq \li a_{n} + \li b_{n}\),

    • (d) if \(a_{n}, b_{n} \geq 0\) for all \(\nN , \ls (a_{n}b_{n}) \leq \left (\ls a_{n}\right ) \left (\ls b_{n}\right )\),

    • (e) if \(a_{n}, b_{n} \geq 0\) for all \(\nN , \li (a_{n}b_{n}) \geq \left (\li a_{n}\right )\left (\li b_{n}\right ),\)

    • (f) \(\ls |a_{n}| = 0 \Rightarrow (a_{n})\) converges to \(0\).

  • 2.3 Let \((S, \Sigma )\) be a measurable space and \(f:S \rightarrow \R \) be a constant function, i.e. there exists \(c \in \R \) so that \(f(x) = c\) for all \(x \in S\). Show that \(f\) is measurable.

  • 2.4 If \((S, \Sigma )\) be a measurable space and \(f:S \rightarrow \R \) show that \(f\) is measurable if and only if \(f^{-1}((a,b)) \in \Sigma \) for all \(-\infty \leq a < b \leq \infty \).

  • 2.5 Let \((S, \Sigma )\) be a measurable space and \(f:S \rightarrow \R \) be a measurable function.

    • (a) Show that \(g = f + c\) is measurable, where \(c \in \R \) is fixed,

    • (b) Show that \(g = kf\) is measurable, where \(k \in \R \) is fixed.

  • 2.6 Let \((S, \Sigma )\) be a measurable space and \(f:S \rightarrow \R \) be a measurable function. If \(g:\R \rightarrow \R \) is Borel measurable, show that \(g \circ f\) is measurable from \(S\) to \(\R \). What does this result tell us about functions of random variables in probability theory?

  • 2.7 Let \(f:\R \rightarrow \R \) be Borel measurable. Show that the mapping \(h:\R \rightarrow \R \) is measurable, where \(h(x) = f(x+y)\) for all \(x \in \R \), and where \(y \in \R \) is fixed.

  • 2.8 Let \((S, \Sigma )\) be a measurable space and \(f:S \rightarrow \R \) be a function. Define the function \(|f|: S \rightarrow \R \) by \(|f|(x) = |f(x)|\) for all \(x \in \R \). Show that

    • (a) \(f = f_{+} - f_{-}\),

    • (b) \(|f| = f_{+} + f_{-}\),

    • (c) if \(f\) is measurable then \(|f|\) is also measurable.

  • 2.9 Let \(f:\R \rightarrow \R \) be a differentiable function. Explain why both \(f\) and its derivative \(f^{\prime }\) are measurable functions.

  • 2.10 Let \(f:\R \rightarrow \R \) be monotonic increasing. Show that it is measurable.

  • 2.11 Let \((S, \Sigma )\) be a measure space and \((f_{n})\) be a sequence of measurable functions from \(S\) to \(\R \). Let \(f:S \rightarrow \R \) be measurable. We say that \(f_{n} \rightarrow f\) almost everywhere or (a.e.) for short, if \(\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty }f_{n}(x) = f(x)\) for all \(x \in S-A\) where \(m(A) = 0\). Show that if \(f_{n} \rightarrow f\) (a.e.) and \(g_{n} \rightarrow g\) (a.e.) then

    • (a) \(f_{n}^{2} \rightarrow f^{2}\) (a.e.)

    • (b) \(f_{n} + g_{n} \rightarrow f + g\) (a.e.)

    • (c) \(f_{n}g_{n} \rightarrow fg\) (a.e.)

Challenge questions

  • 2.12 A function \(f:\R \rightarrow \R \) is said to be upper-semicontinuous at \(x \in \R \), if given any \(\epsilon > 0\) there exists \(\delta > 0\) so that \(f(y) < f(x) + \epsilon \) whenever \(|x -y| < \delta \).

    • (a) Show that \(f = {\1}_{[a, \infty )}\) (where \(a \in \R \)) is upper-semicontinuous for all \(x \in \R \),

    • (b) Deduce that the floor function \(f(x) = \lfloor x \rfloor \), which is equal to the greatest integer less than or equal to \(x\), is upper-semicontinuous at all \(x \in \R \).

    • (c) Show that if \(f\) is upper-semicontinuous for all \(x \in \R \) then \(f\) is measurable.
